Fighting Basics

by: Tyler Cheng

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

I decided to make this game because I like playing fighting games. I also wanted to try to challenge myself to make a two-player game, which most people do not do. I also thought that I could make it simple enough to be easy to make.

2. What I Learned

I learned that the two-player part of the game can lead to some unfixable glitches. While some classmates and teachers told me that two players would not work, I decided to stick with the original idea.

3. Architecture of My Game

The game has two players that can attack each other. The main classes are the runner, panel, players, attacks, and manager. The manager detects hits, the players are what the people control and have hurtboxes, the attacks contain hitboxes that affect the players, the panel contains all the visual parts, and the runner contains the main() method.

4. Technical Implementation

I am proud of still making the two-player aspect of the game work, despite it being difficult to make work. I made it work by arranging the code in a specific way. I also made the player 2 health bar decrease on the left side instead of the right by increasing the x-value for every hit.

5. Most Challenging Part

The most difficult part was that I was facing constant bugs and glitches in my code. I solved them by asking for help and figuring them out problem by problem.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

I would like to complexify the combat of the game (add shielding, hitstun, more attacks, jumping, etc.)
